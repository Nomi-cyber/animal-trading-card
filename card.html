Lion

The lion is a species in the family Felidae; it is a muscular, deep-chested cat with a short, rounded head, a reduced neck and round ears, and a hairy tuft at the end of its tail.


  • Scientific Name: Pathera Leo
  • Average Length: 1.7 to 2.5m
  • Average Lifespan: 10-14 Years
  • Habitat: Lions live in different habitats in Asia and Africa

Lions are the second largest big cat species in the world.The average male lion weighs around 180 kg (400 lb) while the average female lion weighs around 130 kg (290 lb). Lions can reach speeds of up to 81 kph (50 mph) but only in short bursts because of a lack of stamina. Lions are very social compared to other cat species, often living in prides that feature females, offspring and a few adult males.
